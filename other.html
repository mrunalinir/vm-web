WHOSE AD IS IT ANYWAY?

Event Overview

Ads are everywhere. More often than not, they define a brand. In this event, participants will be asked to identify brands from their minimalist ad campaigns, in under one minute.

Rules of Procedure

  • Each participant gets one minute to identify as many brands as they can, consecutively.
  • The questions are divided into sets of easy, medium and difficult with +1, +2 and +3 points respectively.
  • The participant can choose the difficulty of each question that they wish to attempt.
  • However, if the participant is unable to answer correctly, they are instantly eliminated. (sudden death)
  • A turn ends either when the participant answers incorrectly or when one minute is over, whichever happens first.
  • The partcipant(s) with the maximum points win(s).

BY THE LAW

Event Overview

Laws are important because they act as a guideline as to what is acceptable in a society. Their absence would create conflicts and chaos. Once in a while certain laws are passed that entirely change the perception of the world. These can be called revolutionary laws. Some noteworthy example include Section 377, Gun control etc.

n this event participants would be given a set of prompts based on these laws. The participants are required to select one of them and write an article on that particular law. The participants will be judged on the basis of their writing skills, grammar and their overall assessment of the law.

YOUTH MAHASABHA

Event Overview

For good ideas and true innovation, you need human interaction, conflict, argument and debate.

Youth Mahasabha is a platform for young minds from colleges all over India to find a way to celebrate diversity of thoughts and debate our differences. Come fuel your desire to debate and to stand for yourself when the stakes are high.

Rules of Procedure

  • The language to be used is Hindi.
  • Participants are made to discuss on a topic or subject for a limited time. The moderator keeps the discussion on track and ensures no deviation from the topic.
  • 3 minutes for a speech in favour or against
  • 2 minutes will be given to each contestant to question opponent.
